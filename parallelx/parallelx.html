The Taj Mahal, located in Agra, India, is one of the most famous monuments in the world. It was built by the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal, who died in 1631. This white marble mausoleum is a symbol of eternal love and stands as an architectural masterpiece of the Mughal era. Construction began in 1632 and took over 20 years to complete, involving thousands of artisans and craftsmen. The Taj Mahal is known for its symmetric beauty, intricate carvings, and stunning inlay work using semi-precious stones. The central dome is about 73 meters high and is flanked by four elegant minarets. The monument is surrounded by beautiful gardens, fountains, and reflecting pools, adding to its charm.
